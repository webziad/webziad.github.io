Present perfect ( I have done)
Tom can't find his key.
He 's lost his key. => He has lost...
He has lost his key= he lost it and he dees'nt have it now.
have lost / has lost is the present perfect simple.
I/we/they/you finished,lost
he/she/it done,been,gone

The present perfect simple is have/has +past participle.The past participle often ends in -ed (finished/decided etc..),but many verbs are irregular(lost/done/written etc...).
for a list of irregular verrbs click here.

When we say something has happened,this is usually new information:


  • Ow! I've cut my finger
  • The road is closed. there's been and accident. = (There has been..
  • He told me his name,but I've forgotten it
  • Sally is still she hasn't goneout yet.
  • I can't find my bag. Have you seenit

    • compare gone to and been to

    • James is on holiday.He has gone to Italy =(he is there now or on his way there.
    • Amy is back home now.she has been to Italy =(she has now come back)

    you can use the present perfect with just,already,yet

    just = > a short time ago:

    • Are you hungry? 'no, i've just had lunch'
    • Hello. Have you just arrived?

    Already => sooner than expected.

    • Don't forget to pay the bill. I've already paidit
    • What time is Mark leaving? he's already left.
    Yet = > until now. we use yet to show that we are expecting sth to happen. we use yet in questions and negative sentences:
    • Has it stopped raining yet?
    • I've written the email,but I I haven't send it yet.
